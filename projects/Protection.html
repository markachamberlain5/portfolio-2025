Modernising Protection Workflows

Simplifying the complex underwriting journey to help advisors secure protection policies faster and with fewer errors.

2. Gamifying the 'Fact Find'

Financial Fact Finds usually feel like interrogations. I shifted the dynamic by introducing an interactive drag-and-drop interface. Clients rank their concerns (e.g., "Paying the mortgage if I die") in real-time. This not only engages the client but allows the system to automatically prioritise the correct product logic in the background.

3. Transparency First: The Budget Slider

Advisors often waste time generating quotes that clients can't afford. We introduced a "Budget First" approach. By setting a monthly cap before sourcing, the system filters out unrealistic options, ensuring the conversation remains focused on value rather than "sticker shock."

4. Live Policy Tuning

Traditionally, changing a quote meant re-submitting a form and waiting for a server response. I designed this "Equaliser" interface using local state management. Advisors can slide the coverage amounts up and down (e.g., decreasing Term Assurance to increase Critical Illness) and see the premium update instantly.

5. Visualising Value: Good, Better, Best

Complex insurance packages are hard to compare. We moved away from a spreadsheet view to a card-based layout. This allows advisors to present a "Single Policy" solution alongside a "Multi-Benefit" solution, clearly visualising the extra coverage a client gets for a marginal increase in premium.

6. Aggregated Provider Marketplace

The backend integrates APIs from major providers like L&G, LV=, and The Exeter. The UI normalises this data, presenting disparate product features in a unified, consistent format so advisors can compare "apples to apples" without logging into five different provider portals.

7. Interaction Design Details

Micro-interactions matter. The sliders use clear visual cues (orange bars) to show coverage duration relative to the mortgage term. This visual feedback loop helps advisors spot gaps in protection instantly (e.g., if the policy ends 5 years before the mortgage is paid off).

8. Holistic Financial View

Protection isn't just about life insurance. We integrated General Insurance (Buildings & Contents) into the same sourcing flow. By presenting these alongside the main protection quote (e.g., via Uinsure), we increased cross-sell rates and ensured clients were fully covered from day one.

9. Zero-Error Data Entry

Bad data kills applications. We implemented smart address lookups and field validation that runs before submission. This reduced NIGO (Not In Good Order) rates significantly, ensuring that when an application goes to an underwriter, it goes through first time.

The Challenge

Protection advice (Life Insurance, Critical Illness) is notoriously complex. Advisors were struggling with a disjointed workflow that required them to switch between a legacy CRM, external provider portals (like Aviva/L&G), and manual Excel calculators. This friction led to data entry errors, slow application processing, and ultimately, a risk of clients dropping out before cover was secured.

Research & Insights

Observing the advisors revealed three critical bottlenecks:

  • 1 Double Keying: Advisors were typing the same medical data into three different systems.
  • 2 Compliance Anxiety: Advisors were terrified of missing a mandatory disclosure question, leading them to over-document in unstructured notes.
  • 3 Quote Comparison: Comparing multi-benefit quotes (e.g., Life + CIC vs. Life only) was manual and hard to present to clients visually.

Design Solution

1. The "Single Truth" Fact Find

I designed a unified data capture flow that sits on top of the provider APIs. Advisors enter client health data once, and the system formats it for multiple underwriting engines automatically. This eliminated re-keying errors entirely.

2. Smart Disclosure Logic

Instead of asking advisors to guess which products suited a client, I created a logic matrix (shown below) that mapped natural language concerns directly to technical product outcomes. This ensured that if a client mentioned "worrying about the mortgage," the system automatically prioritised Mortgage Protection logic over generic Life Insurance.

Logic Matrix: Mapping Client Concerns to Products

Snapshot of the data model used to drive the underwriting engine.

Client Concern / Need Product Protection Type Key Variable Logic
If I were to pass away, I would want to ensure the mortgage is paid off. Life Mortgage Ben Amount = Mort amount
I would like to leave an inheritance or legacy for dependents if I pass away. WOL (Whole of Life) Family LTA (Long Term Asset)
I would like medical expenses to be covered if I was diagnosed with a serious illness. CIC (Critical Illness) Family Value = LCIC
I would like to make sure that my mortgage would be paid off if I became seriously ill. CIC Mortgage Ben Term = Mort term
If I couldn't work due to illness or injury, I would need to replace lost income. IP/AS (Income Prot) Family Ben Term = Yrs to retirement
I would like to provide a regular income for my dependents if I were to pass away. FIB (Family Income Ben) Family Term reduced to kids age 21
If I lost everything in my home, I wouldn't be able to afford to replace it. Contents General Insurance Value = Sum insured

3. Visual Quote Comparison

I created a "Good, Better, Best" visual comparison tool. This allowed advisors to present different protection packages to clients side-by-side on screen, making the value of comprehensive cover easier to explain.
