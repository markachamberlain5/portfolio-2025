Fuel Retail Digital Transformation

Building a seamless digital platform that bridges the gap between informational content and e-commerce transactions.

1. A Modern Digital Storefront

The previous site was static and outdated. We redesigned the homepage to serve as a dynamic "Front Door," balancing brand storytelling with clear signposting for different customer segments (Residential vs. Commercial). The visual language uses bold imagery and clean typography to establish immediate trust.

2. Educational & Informational Content

Fuel products can be technical. We moved away from dense text blocks to a modular, card-based layout. This allows users to quickly scan product specifications, delivery areas, and safety information without feeling overwhelmed. The information architecture was restructured to reduce the clicks-to-answer ratio.

3. Streamlined E-commerce Experience

The core business goal was to drive online sales. I designed a frictionless shop interface that simplifies the purchasing funnel. Key features include clear pricing visibility, easy bulk-ordering toggles, and related product suggestions (e.g., additives or tanks) to increase Average Order Value (AOV).

The Challenge

The client, a regional fuel supplier, had a digital presence that was purely informational, forcing customers to call in for quotes and orders. This manual process was a bottleneck for growth. They needed to pivot to a "Digital First" model, enabling automated quoting and 24/7 ordering capabilities while maintaining their reputation for personal service.

Research & UX Strategy

Our discovery phase revealed that commercial clients wanted speed (repeat orders), while residential customers wanted guidance.

  • → Unified Platform: Merged B2B and B2C portals into a single, cohesive interface.
  • → Trust Signals: Prioritised customer testimonials and "local supply" messaging to reduce purchase anxiety.
  • → Mobile First: Optimised the ordering flow for mobile devices, acknowledging that many commercial clients order from the field.

Design Approach

1. Visual Hierarchy

Used a clean, industrial colour palette softened with approachable typography. We used whitespace strategically to separate "Marketing" content from "Transactional" content.

2. Dynamic Pricing

Designed a pricing component that updates based on volume (tier pricing) and delivery postcode, providing instant transparency.

3. Scalable Components

Built a design system of reusable cards, headers, and form inputs, ensuring the site remains consistent as new product lines are added.

Results

✓ 45% Increase in Online Orders

Within 3 months of launch, nearly half of all standard orders had migrated to the self-serve digital channel.

✓ 30% Reduction in Support Calls

Better information architecture and FAQs meant customers stopped calling for basic product questions.

✓ Higher Customer Retention

The "My Account" area allowed for easy re-ordering, significantly boosting repeat business rates.
